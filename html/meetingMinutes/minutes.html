G Gianni M G Simonetti Thursday November 19, 1925 at 9 p.m. our regular general meeting is brought to order, presided over by our president Michele Simonetti. The roll call returned the absences of the following officers returned no absent officers. Last meeting’s minutes were approved as read. Federigo Barsanti and Alfredo Tamburi were admitted as new members. After a long discussion regarding the Zini-Vigano’ proposal to host a masquerade ball for Carnevale, with 25% of the proceeds going to the orphan school ‘Artigianelli Matteo Civitali di Lucca’, it is approved with 21 votes in favor and 11 against [over the] the Carli-Tambellini proposal to host a ball without masks. Since the Zini-Vigano’ proposal was approved, the president nominated the following Ball Committee: President Enemia De Santi, Vice President Olinto Cinelli, Treasurer Modesto Zini, Secretary of Correspondence Dino Barontini, Secretary of Finance Emilio Pasquinelli, Head of Arrangement Carlo Silvioni, with Giuseppe Tambellini as assistant, and Adriano Puccetti, Giuseppe Martinelli, Americo Pasquinelli, Alfredo Vannucci, Ermete Vannucci, Ettore Guidotti, G. Baggiani, and Eugenio Merciadri as helpers. The Reception Committee [is thus composed]: Stefano Maffei, Adelmo Dati, Giuseppe Fredianelli, and Giuseppe Gianni. Heads of the Room: Lorenzo Vigano’, Lorenzo Lunardini, and Romeo Maffei. The Health Officers reported that the following members were sick: Fioravante Zini, Adriano Puccetti, and Rizieri Menchetti. As is their duty, the Curators looked over the books of the Secretary of Finance and they found them to be in perfect order. Deposited by the Secretary of Finance: $67.50. In the cashbox today: $4649.76. Having no other affairs [to attend to], the president opened the nominations for officers for the year 1926, of which the following were nominated unanimously: President Enemia De Santi, Vice President Modesto Zini, Secretary of Correspondence Giuseppe Gianni, Secretary of Finance Emilio Pasquinelli, Treasurer Santino Pasquinelli, Health Officers Alfredo Vannucci and Lorenzo Vigano’, and Room Guard Eugenio Merciadri. All that is left to do is to choose three Curators from the following six nominees: Carlo Silvioni, Giuseppe Fredianelli, Adelmo Dati, Michele Simonetti, Olinto Cinelli, and Americo Pasquinelli. Il Seg Il Pres G Gianni M G Simonetti Thursday December 17, 1925 at 9 p.m. our regular general meeting is brought to order, presided over by our president Michele Simonetti. The roll call returned the absences of the following officers: Second Curator Adelmo Dati, Third Curator Lorenzo Lunardini, Health Officer Giuseppe Fredianelli, and Room Guard Eugenio Merciadri. Last meeting’s minutes were approved as read. The two new applicants, Alfredo Tamburi and Federigo Barsanti, were admitted. The president of the Ball [Committee] Enemia De Santi announced that the date of the ball had been fixed for the night of Tuesday February 16th at the Elk Club, and that in order for it to be a success the committee needed the cooperation of everybody. With that, members Michele Simonetti, Carlo Silvioni, Jacopo Sodini, Romeo Maffei, Santino Pasquinelli, Alfredo Vannucci, and Lorenzo Lunardini all volunteered themselves to try and sell the advertising booklets. It was proposed by Jacopo Sodini to send a letter to those members late on payments with their respective amounts due, and that whoever does not settle their debt at the next meeting should be dismissed. Proposed by Santino Pasquinelli and seconded by Abramo Marsili to give $10.00 to the youngster who operates the elevator. Proposed by Sam Marsili and seconded by Olinto Cinelli to provide refreshments to the new administration The following bills were paid: $30.00 to Rizieri Menchetti as a subsidy, $4.00 to Adriano Puccetti as a subsidy, $11.25 for room rent, $27.90 for refreshments at Pasquinelli’s, $35.53 to the Secretary of Finance for one year, $6.93 to the Secretary of Correspondence for three months, $7.10 to the Collector, $9.00 to the Treasurer. Deposited by the Secretary of Finance: $45.50. In the cashbox today: $4553.55. The president declared the vote for the three Curators open, to be chosen from the 6 nominees. [There were] 25 Voters. The elected officers were: First Curator Giuseppe Fredianelli with 16 votes, Second Curator Americo Pasquinelli with 12 votes, and Third Curator Olinto Cinelli with 11 votes. Eugenio Merciadri defeated his opponent Ezio Carli for Room Guard. Having nothing else to attend to, the president declared the meeting adjourned. Time 11:30 p.m. Secretary President G.
