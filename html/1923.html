Welcome to the reading view of the first volume of the Lega Toscana di Protezione's minute log, which spans from January of 1919 to December of 1925; please feel free to browse any of the seven years currently included in our corpus. The original manuscript scans are to the left of the window, with the transcription of the log in the middle, and our translation occupying the rightmost column. This is a current project in progress, so any and all comments on the translation or transcription of the minutes are warmly welcomed. Please contact Zac Enick at zacharyenick@pitt.edu with any feedback on the rendering of the volume.

Note: The transcription in the middle column is documented so as to replicate the original scan as closely as possible, but, as was described in our methodology, bracketed words and phrases and additional punctuation were included in the translation to improve legibility in the target language.

 Thursday August 16, 1923 at 9 p.m. the
usual regular meeting is opened, presided over
by our president Stefano Maffei.
The roll call of the officers returned the following
officers absent: first curator Serafino De Simo,
third curator Ezio Carli, sanitary officer Lorenzo Vigano’,
and collector Jacopo Sodini.
The previous meeting’s minutes were approved as read.
Castruccio Cappellini presented a petition
to remove a given sum from petty cash so that
the banquet for our honorary member Giovanni
Pasquinelli will have a greater attendance.
Another petition was presented by Amerigo Lucchesi
to not remove any sum from petty cash .
After having read the Cappellini petition,
brother Michele Simonetti asked for the floor and
voiced to the assembly that the Cappellini petition
was nice but that it was still possible to hold the banquet without
removing any money and that it will be well attended
regardless. Brother Cappellini asked for the floor and
insisted that removing a given sum
from petty cash would prompt a higher attendance at
the banquet in honor of our honorary member
Giovanni Pasquinelli more highly attended.
After a long discussion in which various members
participated, the president put the petitions
to a vote. The result of the vote was
negative for the Cappellini petition,
with 25 votes against and none in favor.
Brother Amerigo Pasquinelli asked for the floor
and announced his resignation as head of the [Banquet] Committee because
the banquet would not achieve a high enough attendance
without removing any money from the society’s petty cash.
Il presidente all’ora nomina Michele Simonetti
a capo comitato e lui scieglie i seguenti componenti
Olindo Cinelli, Amerigo Lucchesi e B. Pardini.
Il fratello Simonetti acconsente di trovare il
locale adatto e di dare un buon pranzo e che
l’ammontare non superi $5.00
In casso oggi: Totale. 3194.20
Non avendo altro da fare Il presidente dichiara
sciolta la seduta. ore 10.45 P.M.

 It was proposed by Modesto Zini, and seconded by
Martino Barelli, to remove $5 from the cashbox
and to give it as a gift to the
youngster who operates the elevator.
The president put it to a vote and
it passed, but the assembly, being
dissatisfied [with the amount], made a collection and gathered
a total of $11.80.
Having nothing else to attend to, the president opened
the vote of the nominees for officers of
the year 1924.
There were thirty voters.
The president nominated Serafino De Simo,
Enemia de Santi, and Ezio Carli to collect
the ballots in a hat and to then read
the results.
The following officers were elected:
Casimiro Pellegrini as President with 19 votes.
Jacopo Sodini as Vice President with 18 votes
Emilio Pasquinelli as Secretary of Finance
with 15 votes. Carlo Marconi as First Curator
with 28 votes, Martino Borelli as Second Curator
with 16 votes, and Americo Lucchesi as Third Curator
with 14 votes. Eugenio Merciadri as Room Guard
with 17 votes. The rest of the officers were elected
unanimously.
Collected by the secretary of finance: $160.65.
